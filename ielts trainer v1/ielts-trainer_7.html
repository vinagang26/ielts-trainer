Self-check before you call it done
Every "both X and Y" statement uses a plural verb — "both have," never "both has."
Every singular countable noun has an article (a/an/the) — check "government," "problem," "solution," "issue."
Collective/uncountable nouns (government, news, information, research) take singular verbs.
After modals (should, can, must) — bare infinitive, no "to." After causatives (make, let) — same rule.
Passive voice checked: "should be taught," not "should teach," when the subject doesn't do the action.
Word forms match their grammatical slot — noun vs. adjective vs. adverb (economic/economical, effect/affect).
